The Truth of God

A Personal Journey Through Faith

Out of curiosity, I’ve spent a lot of time exploring different churches and talking to people from many faiths. I’ve asked a lot of questions—about belief, God, and how religion works. But the more I learn, the more contradictions I see. Often, answers often lead to more confusing questions rather than clarity.

Maybe real faith isn’t about blindly following rules—it’s about finding your own path and discovering what God’s truth really is.

The Core Beliefs and Their Contradictions

Most believers I’ve met share a few big ideas: God is perfect, all-powerful, and loves every single person like His own child. Sounds great, right? But here’s where it gets tricky. If God’s perfect and all-powerful, why’s the world so messed up? If He loves everyone, why do so many people suffer—war, abuse, starvation? I’ve heard some answers, but they don’t quite hold up.

“If God is perfect, why is the world God created so imperfect?”

Most believers say God is perfect, all-powerful, and loves everyone. But if that’s true, why is there so much suffering in the world?

“Satan Caused Suffering”

Some say the world was perfect at first, and suffering came from Satan. But why doesn’t God stop Satan? Isn’t God stronger? And didn’t God create Satan in the first place?

A loving parent wouldn’t stand by while their child gets hurt. Why would a loving God?

“Do we suffer so we can learn good from evil?”

Another answer I’ve heard is, “Suffering and evil exist so we can learn what’s good and what’s bad.” But if God loves everyone equally, why do some people suffer way more than others? Why do some face abuse or famine while others live comfy lives and “learn” from their pain? That’s not fair, and it contradicts the idea that God loves everyone the same. A loving God wouldn’t pick and choose who suffers to teach others a lesson.

Some folks compare suffering to getting a vaccine or working out—temporary pain for a greater good. But that’s a stretch. Vaccines are proven to help, bullying, war, or abuse? They’re just destructive, no benefits. No sane parent would let their kid endure that kind of pain to “learn” anything. And even if vaccines count as suffering, everyone gets them—fair’s fair. But not everyone faces persecution or starvation. That’s not equal at all.

The “Original Sin” Excuse

Some blame Adam and Eve and the idea of original sin. But honestly, the logic of that story doesn’t hold up either. I wrote more on that in: [ Analyzing Genesis 3: Evidence the Bible Does Not Reflect God's True Words or Will].

“Free Will to Choose Good or Evil”

Then there’s the free will argument: God gave us the freedom to choose good or evil. Cool, but what about heaven? Believers say heaven’s perfect, so no evil there, right? Does that mean angels in heaven can’t choose evil? If so, heaven has no free will, which contradicts the whole “free will is God’s gift” idea. Plus, if there’s good and evil with or without God, and God doesn’t stop disasters or Satan, what’s the point of God? It’s like He’s sitting it out, letting us deal with the mess.

I think the “free will to choose good or evil” line isn’t God’s truth—it’s more like a trap, maybe even idea corrupted by Satan. It makes people shrug off evil, like it’s just part of the deal, instead of fighting it. If there’s a loving God, His truth would be: We have free will to choose goodness in all its forms, but choosing evil means you’re under Satan’s influence. That feels more like a God who cares.

“Suffering Makes Life Interesting?”

Some believers ask, “Would you want to live your whole life in a safe house with plenty of food, but no challenges?” That’s mixing up challenge with suffering. A life without pain doesn’t mean you’re locked in a room. You can have an amazing life full of adventure, discovery, and love—without going through trauma or horror. Plenty of wealthy people’s kids live safe, exciting lives. And even their worst days are much better than a life full of pain.

So, Is There a God? And What’s His Truth?

Nobody can prove God exists—or doesn’t. But if there’s a God, God isn't flawless or omnipotent as traditionally portrayed, or maybe His power is matched by Satan’s, and they’re duking it out in ways we can’t see. Maybe their battle ended in a tie, and their spirits got mixed into us humans. When we choose justice and kindness, we’re boosting God’s power; when we choose evil, we’re feeding Satan’s. God might want us to fight as part of Him on Earth—to wipe out injustice and spread love, not just sit back and wait for Him to fix everything.

If God’s real, His truth isn’t about waiting for Him to judge the bad guys or end all suffering. That’s too passive, like something a controlling ruler—or even Satan—might want us to believe to keep us from acting. If we’re God’s children, or part of His spirit, we have to take up the responsibility of judgment too -- fight for justice, call out evil, and make the world better. That’s the truth of God—not hoping for a divine bailout, but taking responsibility to battle injustice , we grow the power of righteousness and faith , right here, right now.

Faith, Science, and the Truth

One day, these ideas just hit me, like a lightbulb going off. Could this be God’s truth speaking to me? I’m not saying angels showed up or God whispered in my ear—nothing like that. I’m just thinking it through. And if God’s real, I bet His truth includes a scientific spirit:

  • Rationality: Use reason and logic, not superstition or blind emotion.
  • Empiricism: Base your beliefs on evidence, observation, and testing.

Without scientific evidence, no one can honestly say they’re 100% sure God exists, so when believers say they’re 100% sure God exists, I’m skeptical. Either they’ve got cushy lives and don’t know real suffering, or they haven’t dug deep enough into God’s truth.

Hope, Unity, and Justice

Even without proof, we hope God is real, and His power—justice, kindness, compassion—is in all of us.

An individual alone is never perfect — but together, we can strive toward perfection.

A single person has limited strength — but united, we hold limitless power.

 

Let us rise together, hand in hand, heart with heart.

Let us spread love and compassion, lighting the path toward a better world.

Let us stand as one against tyranny, oppression, and injustice, so that all people may breathe the air of freedom.

Let us unite — to fight for justice, to drive back the darkness, to ignite the flame of hope in every corner of the earth.

Let us gather the strength of righteousness and kindness, build a force so powerful that even evil must tremble.

 

And when that day comes —when justice triumphs, when truth shines, when love prevails —perhaps we shall see God again.
